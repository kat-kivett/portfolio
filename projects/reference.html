About the Analyst

Thank you for taking the time to explore my portfolio! In this section I'll give some additional background into the creation of this portfolio and who I am as an analyst.

When I'm not spending my evenings creating this portfolio I enjoy spending time with my family, reading, cooking, walking the dog, watching the birds by our bird feeders, and trying to keep my houseplants alive.

Education and Experience

This isn't a resume, but I'd be remiss without including a small blurb about my background. I received my BBA in Management, Magna Cum Laude, from Boise State. I hold a Lead Data Analyst role with a major insurance company, and with a previous role in retail analytics. My areas of focus have ranged from sales and service, people and benefits analytics, accounting and cashflow, and inventory management. I've had the great privilege of working from the top to the bottom of the sales funnel, from top line to bottom line, and everything in between.

I love to solve problems, and I love to teach others how to solve them too. It is easy to think on roadblocks in the workplace as the uncontrollable things: the tools available are inadequate, the data is a mess, it doesn't refresh when I'd like, etc. In reality the roadblocks are usually other items entirely: lack of expertise and creativity in using the tools we have, inability to connect with business partners to understand their needs (WHY am I pulling this for you, WHAT are you doing with this once it is complete, HOW can this data inform follow up questions or projects), or fear of putting yourself out there and trying a new approach.

My best work has come from the unasked question, and by pulling on loose threads. Data Analysis is asking "What If?" and then finding out.

Why

Why not?

Creating this portfolio has been a great exercise in learning and stretching myself. It was a fantastic learning experience, and gave me the opportunity to pick up new skills (HTML, css) and more thoughfully apply those that I already have (database structure, SQL, Python, Storytelling with Data principles). The process from visualization of the whole to the actual creation was an exercise in itself.

Growth Mindset

In any career revolving around data, technology, and visualization there will be constant change. Change is inevitable, and it is impossible for any one person to keep up with everything. The best thing to do is to continue to learn and grow, and to humbly say "I don't know, but I'm going to figure it out." I strongly believe that any new skill learned is a benefit, even if that skill becomes obsolete. I may never again need to know how to adjust the margins of an item class in HTML by digging into the css code, but that's ok. The lesson learned is in debugging, troubleshooting, and critically thinking through an issue.
